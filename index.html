Research

Questions grounded in spectroscopy, coordination, and solar activity.

The work centres on how elemental composition evolves through active regions, flares, and coordinated observing campaigns, and on how those changes can be measured reliably across different instruments and timescales.

Theme 01

Coronal composition and the FIP effect

I study how elemental abundances evolve in active regions and transient events, with particular attention to fractionation signatures seen in EUV spectroscopy.

  • Elemental abundance diagnostics in flare and quiet-Sun environments
  • Composition evolution through solar activity cycles and active regions
  • Links between coronal fractionation and lower-atmosphere processes

Theme 02

Flare dynamics and spectroscopic diagnostics

Recent work examines non-thermal velocity evolution, reconnection outflows, and other flare-related signatures that can be traced in spectroscopic data before and during energy release.

  • Pre-flare behaviour in Hinode/EIS spectroscopy
  • Spatially resolved composition changes in flare structures
  • Interpretation of line broadening and plasma turbulence

Theme 03

Coordinated observations, data products, and instrumentation

I contribute to joint campaigns that connect space- and ground-based facilities, and I publish reusable data products to support comparative studies of solar variability.

  • Solar Orbiter, Hinode, and IRIS coordination
  • Joint campaigns with JVLA, GREGOR, and SST
  • Public datasets for flare spectroscopy and footpoint analysis

Selected publications

Recent first-author papers and current directions.

A fuller bibliography is available on the publications page and in the PDF CV. The selection below foregrounds current research threads rather than a complete record.

Accepted | Philosophical Transactions A

Chromospheric dynamics and turbulence regulate the solar FIP effect

With J. M. Laming, J. Reep, and A. J. Finley.

A current first-author study on how chromospheric dynamics and turbulence regulate fractionation signatures seen higher in the solar atmosphere.

2025 | The Astrophysical Journal

Systematic non-thermal velocity increase preceding soft X-ray flare onset

With A. Burden, D. Baker, H. Eklund, D. H. Brooks, and collaborators.

A large-scale Hinode/EIS study identifying a systematic spectroscopic increase before flare onset across a broad event sample.

2024 | Astronomy and Astrophysics

Spatially resolved plasma composition evolution in a solar flare

With D. H. Brooks, S. Imada, R. J. French, L. van Driel-Gesztelyi, and collaborators.

A flare-focused paper tracing composition changes through reconnection outflow regions with spatially resolved analysis.

2023 | The Astrophysical Journal

Understanding the relationship between solar coronal abundances and F10.7 cm radio emission

With A. W. James, T. S. Bastian, L. van Driel-Gesztelyi, and collaborators.

A study connecting coronal abundance diagnostics with the long-used radio proxy and testing how robustly the two track one another.

2021 | The Astrophysical Journal

The evolution of plasma composition during a solar flare

With D. M. Long, D. Baker, D. H. Brooks, L. van Driel-Gesztelyi, J. M. Laming, and G. Valori.

The first first-author paper in this area, following composition changes through a solar flare and establishing a longer-running research thread.
